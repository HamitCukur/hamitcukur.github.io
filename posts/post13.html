the modern man's guide to strategic investing

01-13-2024

if you're following crypto influencers for investment advice, you might as well consult a magic 8-ball. at least it won't try to sell you a course. üòè

as an engineer, i approach investing the same way i approach designing a mechanical system: with careful analysis, redundancy planning, and a healthy dose of "what's the worst that could happen?" calculations.

the engineer's investment framework:

1. system analysis
before investing in a company, analyze it like a machine. what are its moving parts? where are the potential failure points? is the CEO just a fancy paperweight?

2. risk management
diversification isn't just a buzzword - it's like having multiple backup systems. you wouldn't build a rocket with just one parachute, would you? (looking at you, crypto bros)

3. performance optimization
monitor your portfolio like you'd monitor a production line. identify bottlenecks, optimize for efficiency, and for god's sake, stop checking it every 5 minutes.

common investment myths debunked:

"time in the market beats timing the market"
finally, something warren buffett and i agree on. though i suspect he doesn't use matlab to analyze his investments.

"high risk = high reward"
ah yes, the same logic that leads engineers to say "let's skip the testing phase, what could go wrong?"

the strategic investor's toolkit:

- spreadsheets (because of course)
- patience (harder to master than calculus)
- emotional control (yes, even when elon tweets)

investment red flags:

- if it promises 1000% returns, it's probably a scam
- if your uber driver recommends it, it's definitely a scam
- if it involves a digital monkey jpeg, just... no

remember: investing is like engineering - the goal is to build something that lasts, not something that looks flashy in the demo but crashes in production.
